Pamphobeteus platyomma, Brazilian Pink Bloom

Characteristics

New World species use urticating hairs for defense. They kick those irritating, barbed hairs from their abdomens when stressed or threatened. New World species tend to have weaker venom than their Old World counterparts, with many bits being compared to bee stings in terms of pain and intensity. All of the so-called docile or handleable species are New World species. That said, larger South America genera like Phormictopus and Pamphobeteus can be quite defensive and quick to bite.
